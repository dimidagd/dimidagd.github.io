Driving scene image segmentation using Fully Convolutional Networks

Motivation

Self-driving cars are one of the hottest areas of research and business for the tech giants. What seemed like a science-fiction, a few years ago, now seems more like something which is soon to become a part and parcel of life. “soon to be” is because of the fact that even though companies like Tesla, Nissan, Cadillac do have self-driving car assistance software. They still require a human to keep an eye on the road and take control when needed. However, it is fascinating to see how far we have come in terms of innovation and how fast technology is advancing.

Other applications of semantic segmentation include and definetely not limited to: - Classification of terrain visible in satellite imagery - Medical imaging analysis - Industrial inspection

Background

Fully Convoluted Networks (FCN) for semantic segmentation 1 can be used for semantic segmentation as well. In this project, we will investigate the method described in the paper to perform segmentation on the driving scene image. The framework used is Pytorch.

FCN extends the classic convolutional network pipeline such such that it can be used for full image predictions. FCN takes an image and outputs a classification map. The initial layers are similar to a standard CNN architecture where the image is transformed into a latent represatation that includes contextual information but loses structural information.

Having ground truth available, the network can be trained end to end. A pretrained image classifier was used as the backbone of the pipeline, the design aims to use learned parameters to upsample the low-dimensional output of the convolutional layers to class predictions for every pixel in the original image. The basic method resembles the encoder-decoder architecture, but while making use of the rich contextual information at the output of each subsequent convolutional layers, by creating additional skip connections to the upsampling layers. Both non-learned methods and learned upscaling methods are used to compute the final class predictions on the image pixels.

Dataset:

Camvid and Cityscape are free available labeled dataset.

  1. Cambridge-driving Labeled Video Database (CamVid) is a collection of video and images where each pixel is labelled with one of the 32 semantic labels. This dataset contains both fix-perspective videos (CCTV footage) and from a driving automobile. The footage from automobile makes this dataset a suitable choice for this project.

  1. Cityscape dataset contains labelled video and images from fix-perspective videos in the streets of different cities. The images are labelled with one of the 32 semantic labels. For the project, we would focus on the CamVid dataset recorded from automobile. Fix-perspective videos will not be investigated.

Training:

Different submodels of the original architecture were tested depending on the resolution of the information extracted from the convolutional layers. Namely FCN32s, FCN16s, FCN8s.

The backbone of the network are the pretrained layers conv1,2,3, they are being initialized from the popular VGGnet . Given that VGG was trained on over 1million pictures, these layers are considered good feature extractors.
